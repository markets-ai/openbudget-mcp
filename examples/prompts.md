# Example prompts

Once OpenBudget is connected, ask in plain language — your assistant selects the right tool. These work across Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Codex, and any other MCP client.

## Spending analysis

- "How much did I spend on restaurants last month, and how does it compare to the month before?"
- "What are my top 10 merchants by total spend this year?"
- "Show me every charge over $200 in the last 90 days."
- "How much do I actually spend on coffee?"

## Trips & one-off events

- "What did the Denver trip in March cost me all-in — flights, hotel, food, rideshares?"
- "Total up everything related to the kitchen renovation."
- "How much did the holidays cost me in December?"

## Subscriptions & recurring charges

- "Find every subscription I'm paying for and total them per month."
- "Which recurring charges went up in price this year?"
- "Am I still paying for anything I haven't used in months?"

## Net worth & balances

- "What's my net worth across all accounts right now?"
- "How much cash do I have across checking and savings?"
- "Which accounts haven't synced recently?"

## Debt & liabilities

- "What's the APR on each of my credit cards, and when are the next payments due?"
- "How much principal vs interest have I paid on the mortgage this year?"
- "When did my card's APR last change?"
- "What's my total debt, and how has it trended over the last six months?"

## Income

- "How much income landed last month, and from where?"
- "Compare my income to my spending for each of the last three months."

## Cleanup & rules

- "Venmo payments to Elena are childcare, not transfers — make that a rule."
- "Recategorize that Costco charge as groceries and note it was for the party."
- "Hide the transfers between my own accounts from spending searches."
- "What categorization rules do I have, and what has each one applied to?"

## Budgeting

- "Build me a monthly budget based on my actual spending over the last 6 months."
- "Where am I over budget this month?"
- "If I cut dining out by half, how much would I save per year?"
